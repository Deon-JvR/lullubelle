Consumer Data Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 2026

Lullubelle respects customer privacy and processes personal information in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013, commonly known as POPIA.

  • Email addresses.
  • Telephone numbers.
  • Delivery and billing addresses.
  • Order and transaction information.
  • Customer communication and support enquiries.
  • Website usage, browser and device information.
  • Marketing preferences.
  • Information required to prevent fraud or comply with legal obligations.

How We Use Personal Information

Personal information may be used to:

  • Create and manage customer orders.
  • Process payments.
  • Arrange product delivery.
  • Communicate order and delivery updates.
  • Respond to customer enquiries.
  • Provide customer support.

  • Improve the website, products and customer experience.
  • Send marketing communication where the customer has consented or where permitted by law.

Payment Information

Lullubelle does not intentionally store customers’ complete payment card details.

Payments are processed by third-party payment service providers. Their handling of payment information may be governed by their own privacy policies and security standards.

Sharing Personal Information

Lullubelle may share personal information only where reasonably necessary with service providers such as:

  • Payment processors.
  • Courier and delivery companies.
  • Website hosting and technology providers.
  • Email and communication providers.
  • Professional advisers.
  • Government, regulatory or law-enforcement authorities where legally required.

Lullubelle does not sell customers’ personal information.

The website may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Improve the customer experience.

Data Security

Lullubelle must use reasonable technical and organisational safeguards to protect personal information against loss, misuse, unauthorised access, alteration or disclosure.

Access to customer information must be limited to authorised persons and service providers who require it for legitimate business purposes.

Personal information must only be retained for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including legal, accounting, tax, fraud-prevention and dispute-resolution requirements.
